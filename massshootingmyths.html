Project Overview

Course: Design and Code
Professor: Dr. David Harris Smith
Software: Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator

The Goal

When a high-profile incident such as a mass shooting occurs, people naturally try to connect the dots and form conclusions. This can result in facts being twisted or misguided correlations being perpetuated. The main goal was to dispel these widespread myths surrounding mass shootings and to clarify what the facts really are in a visually engaging way.

Design Goals:

  • Layout is well organized and the topic is presented in a sensible structure.
  • Elements are simple and clean.
  • Text is concise. Images successfully visualize and support the text.
  • Design theory guides visual decisions.

The Process

Step One: Research.

I began by consulting a variety of resources viewing the issue from multiple perspectives to build an accurate picture of the facts. I initially planned to cover the topic of mass shootings more broadly with a focus on the United States. However, what I discovered during the research process was that proven information is scarce and reserving my research to the United States added another constraint. Despite how common they may seem, mass shootings occur very rarely, so limited resources are allocated toward conducting studies on the topic. I worked around this by narrowing down my idea to focus on myths with a closing section on prevention. I knew I had an adequate amount of information to successfully build the infographic around that.

Research.


Step Two: Brainstorming and Image Creation.

Once I had completed my research, I brainstormed ways to visualize the information. This involved discussions with my peers and professor/teacher's assistants and quick sketches of potential icons I could combine with key words. I then created vector images based on my sketches using Adobe Illustrator.

Brainstorming Sketches.


Step Three: Layout Design.

I experimented with the layout in Adobe InDesign and, after receiving feedback from others, I decided the simple vertical layout was the most balanced and suitable for my content.

Two additional layout designs.


Design Choices:

  • I chose a complementary colour palette consisting of blue and orange to maximize contrast so the key words in the text pop.
  • The sizing of the elements including the text and images was determined using the golden ratio to create a balanced visual hierarchy.
  • The section about the perpetrators is designed to resemble a school, a common target for mass shootings. The line created by the bullet guides the eye through the building.
  • It is true that mass shootings are often unpredictable, but there are steps we can take to reduce the frequency. I chose to make the background a gradient that gradually lightens to reflect the decision to end the infographic with a hopeful section on prevention.
