Project Overview

Course: Design and Code
Professor: Dr. David Harris Smith
Software: Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator

About

For this project, I was to create an infographic on a topic of my choosing. When a high-profile incident such as a mass shooting occurs, people try to connect the dots and form conclusions, leading to facts being twisted or correlations being misinterpreted. My observation of this phenomenon sparked the idea behind my infographic, and I created it with the goal of dispelling widespread myths surrounding mass shootings and clarifying what the facts really are in a visually engaging way.

The Goal

The layout of the infographic should be well organized and the topic should be presented in a structure that is appropriate for the content. The text should be concise and the images should successfully visualize and support the text. The elements should be clean and design theory should guide the visual choices.

Design

Once I completed my research on the topic, I brainstormed ways to organize the information. I started by defining mass shootings, highlighting common misconceptions, focusing on the perpetrators, and finally concluding with a more hopeful section on prevention. Once I laid out my story, I developed sketches of potential icons I could use to visually communicate key terms or data. I then created vector images based on my sketches using Illustrator. I then built these pieces into a vertical layout.

I chose a complementary colour palette consisting of blue and orange to maximize contrast and allow the key words in the text pop. The sizing of the elements including the text and images was determined using the golden ratio to create a balanced visual hierarchy. The section about the perpetrators is designed to resemble a school, one of the most common targets for mass shootings. The line created by the bullet guides the eye through the building. The myths are all marked by a shattered glass effect consistent with the typography of the title. I chose to make the background a gradient that gradually lightens to reflect the decision to end the infographic with the prevention section.
