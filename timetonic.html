About

The Senior Thesis project allowed students in the final year of the Multimedia program to individually conceptualize and execute an advanced creative project under supervision by a professor. During the fall semester, I pitched my project to my classmates and professors, conducted a literature review, wrote a thesis proposal, and created a short video and written project outline as proof of concept. The project was produced throughout the winter. At the end of the semester, I presented my completed project live to my peers and advisors at McMaster's Black Box Theatre.

Concept

The concept of my thesis project was to explore the history of graphic design and observe the effect of design on branding through packaging design for an imaginary beverage. Six “flavours” of the drink correspond to six decades, from the 1960s to the 2010s. The design of each pop can reflects a movement that was influential to the graphic design industry during each decade.

Process & Designs

The first steps were conceptualizing the project, arranging a pitch and writing a proposal. The proposal included a project synopsis, literature review, design specifications including descriptions of key elements and overall plan, a timeline with deadlines, a description of the proof of concept, and a skill assessment. While the project was expected to change and evolve throughout the year, the work in these early stages helped create a sturdy foundation to build the project on. Read my thesis proposal here.

Next was the proof of concept which consisted of my sketches, moodboard, design, and mockup for the first decade (1960s). I also shared a short screen recording showing how I created the design and mockup in Illustrator and Photoshop.

Once I completed all six designs, I created a website similar to this page, along with a slideshow presentation. A short write-up also accompanies each design, providing the history behind each style and their unique design characteristics. The concluding findings of the project were that graphic design does not exist in a vacuum - design trends are a reflection of the cultural climate, and shifts in style often originate from defiance against a previous movement’s conventions or longstanding societal norms. When applied to the same brand, the distinct styles of the decades highlight how differences in formal properties significantly alter what is being communicated by the design.
