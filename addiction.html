Project Overview

Course: Time-Based Media
Professor: Liss Platt
Models: Katrina McMullen and Shelby Crego
Equipment: Nikon D5600
Software: Adobe Lightroom

The Goal

The goal of this project was to tell a story through a sequence of photos by setting up an expectation and thwarting it. Each photo should depict an important scene necessary to move the story forward.

Technical goals:

  • Images are taken with proper exposure, contrast, and colour balance.
  • Images are consistent and match each other in all technical aspects.
  • Colour grading and aesthetics suit the mood of the story.

The Process

Step One: Idea Development.

At the beginning, I had difficulty coming up with a strong concept for this project. Instead of waiting for an idea to come to me, I turned to observing my own life for inspiration. My friends and I had been purchasing bubble tea between classes with increasing frequency. We often joked about our sugary addiction, sparking the idea for my project. I felt the twist of leading the viewer to expect a more sinsister addiction and a final reveal of it merely being bubble tea would work well as a sequence of photos.


Step Two: Brainstorming.

I then started brainstorming ways to approach my photos including possible compositions, locations, and subject matter. I kept my original goals in mind throughout this phase, scrapping any photo ideas I deemed unnecessary to the story. This left me with five photos that each play an essential role in the project.


Step Three: Taking the Photos.

For each photo, I took shots from many different angles so I would have options, allowing me to choose the best photos in post-production.

Step Four: Post-Production.

Using Adobe Lightroom, I edited the RAW files beginning with colour correction and white balance to ensure those aspects were consistent throughout my series of photos. I also made sure the photos were not overly vibrant to adhere to the theme of my project. My locations were already low in vibrancy and the weather was overcast while I was shooting so minimal adjustments were required. I then added a subtle black vignette to each photo, enhancing the darker tone I wanted to portray and the cohesion between them.
